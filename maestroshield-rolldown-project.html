What this example shows

Why someone chooses a rolldown instead of a fixed screen wall

Control matters more than a fixed enclosure feel

Rolldowns make sense when you want the option to open the space back up instead of permanently closing the opening.

Opening size changes the conversation

Larger openings need a different pricing and planning approach than standard screen walls, which is why these jobs have their own estimate path.

The screen has to work with the surrounding structure

On many jobs, the opening is part of a covered patio or roofed enclosure, so the rolldown system has to fit the larger project cleanly.

Good questions to ask on a project like this
  • How many openings need motorized screens?
  • Do the openings all match, or are they different sizes?
  • Is the project near the beach or in stronger wind conditions?
  • Is the opening part of a roofed enclosure, patio cover, or mixed-roof project?
Where this fits
