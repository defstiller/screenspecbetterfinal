Scope matters

Repair, rescreen, rebuild, or build new.

The right answer depends on the structure, the roof system, and how much of the enclosure is still worth keeping.

Good first step

Choose the right starting point.

Repair or rescreen Best when the frame still has life and the job is mainly screen replacement, hardware, or targeted repair.
Rebuild or replace Best when the old enclosure is too worn out to keep fixing one section at a time.
New build, existing-roof screen-in, or Elite Room Best when you are building fresh, screening in a covered patio, or moving into a composite roof, pan roof, or rolldown setup.

Planning support

Base, roof-tie-in, and opening conditions still affect the quote

Even when the enclosure is the main job, these surrounding details still change how the project gets priced and built.

Base and footing conditions

Existing slab, pavers, tile, or missing footing can all change how a new enclosure or Elite Room gets planned and priced. Those conditions need to be part of the quote, not tacked on later.

Existing roof tie-ins

Some projects are screen walls, doors, and rails built under the house roof instead of under a free-standing roof of their own, which changes how the enclosure is measured and priced.

Rolldown openings

MaestroShield™ openings are priced by opening size, system count, and wind exposure, not the same way as fixed screen walls.
