Screen Enclosure Working Notes

What an enclosure is
- A screen enclosure is a framed outdoor structure, usually aluminum, built around a pool, patio, lanai, or covered opening.
- It can be free-standing or tied back into the house / existing roof.
- Main job: keep bugs out, define the outdoor space, and sometimes add shade, weather coverage, privacy, or wind control.

How it works
- Structure: aluminum posts, beams, rails, doors, and screen panels.
- Base: usually slab, pavers, tile over slab, or existing surface. Footing/base conditions affect what can be built.
- Roof: can be screen, solid/composite/pan roof, mixed roof, or an existing house roof with screen walls under it.
- Walls: screened sides; the house itself can act as one side instead of a wall.
- Openings: doors, large spans, or motorized rolldown openings.
- Stability: tie-downs / cables reduce lateral movement and matter in wind-prone Florida conditions.

Useful project types
- Rescreen: frame stays, screen is replaced.
- Repair: targeted fix to frame/hardware/problem areas.
- Rebuild: old enclosure is too far gone; replace structure cleanly.
- New enclosure: full new layout around pool/patio/lanai.
- Existing-roof screen-in: roof already exists; build screen walls/doors below it.
- Roof-system enclosure: composite or pan roof for more shade / rain coverage.
- Rolldown system: motorized screen on openings; different from fixed wall pricing.

Screen / material notes
- 18/14 mesh: standard everyday screen.
- 20/20 mesh: tighter no-see-um bug protection.
- PetScreen: more puncture-resistant, good for pets / high-traffic areas, but bug control can be worse than finer mesh.
- Super Screen: stronger premium screen; useful for larger openings / open-view applications.
- Aluminum screen: older option; generally less desirable than newer materials for durability.
- Stainless fasteners/tapcons: preferred replacement hardware when old fasteners are being changed.

Estimate-sheet term notes
- Screen Enclosure: standard screened structure.
- Elite Room: your term for a composite roof enclosure.
- Slab / Pavers / Tile: ground/base finish under enclosure.
- Additional slab outside enclosure: extra concrete outside main enclosure footprint.
- Open view: fewer visual obstructions / larger openings for cleaner sightlines.
- Enclosure color: usually white or bronze aluminum finish.
- Chair rail: horizontal aluminum rail at lower wall height; can break up panel and support stronger lower screen.
- Kick plate: solid aluminum lower wall panel for protection from damage, pets, tools, etc.
- Door QTY and location: how many doors and where they go.
- Door sizes: common widths shown are 36, 42, 48.
- Pet screen on chair rail: stronger screen only in the lower impact zone.
- Screw covers: cosmetic finish pieces to hide/expose fastener lines more cleanly.
- Hose bib / gutter guards / fan beam / shingles: surrounding conditions or tie-in items that can affect details of the build.

Mental model for quoting / discussing
- First decide: rescreen, repair, rebuild, or new build.
- Then decide: free-standing vs existing-roof vs covered-roof / mixed-roof.
- Then decide: base condition, wall count, door count, mesh/screen type, and add-ons like kick plate or chair rail.
- For rolldowns: think opening size, number of systems, wind exposure, and whether they are part of a larger roofed enclosure.
